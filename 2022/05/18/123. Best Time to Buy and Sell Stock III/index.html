Solution --- 123. Best Time to Buy and Sell Stock III

May 18, 2022 2637

Description

You are given an array prices where prices[i] is the price of a given stock on the ith day.

Find the maximum profit you can achieve. You may complete at most two transactions.

Note: You may not engage in multiple transactions simultaneously (i.e., you must sell the stock before you buy again).

Examples

Example 1:
Input: prices = [3,3,5,0,0,3,1,4]
Output: 6
Explanation: Buy on day 4 (price = 0) and sell on day 6 (price = 3), profit = 3-0 = 3.
Then buy on day 7 (price = 1) and sell on day 8 (price = 4), profit = 4-1 = 3.

Example 2:
Input: prices = [1,2,3,4,5]
Output: 4
Explanation: Buy on day 1 (price = 1) and sell on day 5 (price = 5), profit = 5-1 = 4.
Note that you cannot buy on day 1, buy on day 2 and sell them later, as you are engaging multiple transactions at the same time. You must sell before buying again.

Example 3:
Input: prices = [7,6,4,3,1]
Output: 0
Explanation: In this case, no transaction is done, i.e. max profit = 0.

Constraints:
1 <= prices.length <= 10^5
0 <= prices[i] <= 10^5

Solution

Initialize two 2D arrays, g and l, both of size Nx3, where N is the length of the prices array. g[i][j] represents the maximum profit up to day i with j transactions allowed and the last transaction being a sell. Similarly, l[i][j] represents the maximum profit up to day i with j transactions allowed and the last transaction being a buy.
Iterate through the prices array starting from the second day (index 1). For each day i, calculate the difference between the current day’s price and the previous day’s price.
For each day i and for each possible number of transactions j (from 1 to 2), update l[i][j] and g[i][j] according to the following rules:

  • l[i][j] is updated based on whether it’s more profitable to start a new transaction at day i or extend the previous transaction from day i-1.
  • g[i][j] is updated as the maximum profit obtained so far with j transactions allowed.
    The maximum profit achievable is stored in g[-1][-1], which represents the maximum profit up to the last day with at most two transactions allowed.
